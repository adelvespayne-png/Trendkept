# 01 — The Business Model

## The product in one sentence

A **focused, premium supplement brand** in a single well-defined niche, sold
direct-to-consumer on a **subscription-first** basis, fulfilled by a vetted
white-label manufacturer, with AI used to keep content, support, and operating
costs low.

## Why a *brand*, not a generic dropship store

The instinct with "dropshipping" is to list dozens of random products from a
marketplace and run ads. For supplements this is the **worst** possible model:

- You don't control quality → liability and refunds.
- You don't control the label → compliance violations you can't even see.
- You compete on price against identical listings → margins collapse.
- You never build an asset → no repeat customers, no enterprise value.

Instead we use a **white-label / private-label dropship** structure: a
contract manufacturer produces a proven formula, prints **our** label, and
**ships under our brand** directly to the customer. We still hold little or no
inventory at launch, but we own the brand, the customer, and the margin.

This is the difference between "reselling" and "building something sellable."

## Target customer (pick one, deeply)

Do **not** try to serve everyone. Choose one customer and one job-to-be-done.
Strong candidate niches (validated in file 03):

- **Sleep & recovery** for stressed knowledge workers (e.g. magnesium glycinate,
  L-theanine blends).
- **Daily greens / foundational health** for the 30–50 wellness buyer.
- **Active-lifestyle recovery** (electrolytes, creatine) for amateur athletes.

We pick **one** at launch. Depth beats breadth: one hero product, one or two
complements, one clearly-defined buyer.

## Why subscription-first is the profit engine

The fatal flaw of one-off DTC is that customer acquisition cost (CAC) often
exceeds the profit on a single order. Supplements are *consumable and
habitual* — the customer needs a fresh bottle every 30–60 days. That makes
them one of the best categories for subscriptions.

The economics only work if **lifetime value (LTV) > 3× CAC**. A one-off sale
rarely clears that bar; a subscriber who stays 6–9 months clears it easily.
So the entire funnel is built to convert a first purchase into a subscription.

See file 04 for the actual math, and `tools/unit_economics.py` to test it.

## Revenue streams (in priority order)

1. **Subscriptions** — the core. Discounted vs. one-off to drive sign-up.
2. **One-time purchases** — entry point and gifting; upsold to subscription.
3. **Bundles** — hero + complement, raises average order value (AOV).
4. **(Later) Wholesale / retail** — only once the brand is proven online.

## Positioning

Compete on **trust and specificity**, never on price:

- Third-party tested, CoA published (radical transparency).
- One niche, done properly — "the X people" not "a supplement store."
- Clean, honest labeling and copy (which, conveniently, is also the compliant
  copy — see file 02).

## What this is NOT

- Not a 50-SKU general store.
- Not anonymous AliExpress reselling of ingestibles (genuinely dangerous —
  unknown facilities, no CoA, contamination and adulteration risk).
- Not "passive income while you sleep." It is a real, lean business that AI
  makes runnable by a very small team.
