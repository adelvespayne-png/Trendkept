# 09 — 90-Day Launch Roadmap

> A week-by-week path from zero to a live, compliant, demand-validated brand —
> with **go/no-go gates** so you never pour money or time into the next phase
> until the current one is proven. Adjust dates to your reality; keep the gates.

---

## Phase 0 — Foundation & compliance (Weeks 1–3)
*Goal: be legally able and safe to sell. No selling yet.*

- Choose **one** niche using the scoring grid (file 03).
- Validate demand cheaply: keyword/search volume, competitor scan, a small
  "coming soon" landing page collecting emails.
- Form entity; open business bank account.
- Retain attorney + regulatory consultant.
- Shortlist 3 cGMP white-label suppliers; request samples + CoAs; **independently
  lab-test** samples.
- Draft brand, label artwork → regulatory review.
- Bind product + general liability insurance.
- Draft ToS, Privacy Policy, **compliant subscription/auto-renew terms**.

> **GATE 0 (must pass to continue):** Supplier cGMP + CoA verified · label
> approved · insurance bound · legal docs ready · compliance checklist (file
> 02) fully ticked. *If any item fails, do not proceed — fix or change supplier.*

---

## Phase 1 — Build the store & engine (Weeks 4–6)
*Goal: a working, compliant storefront and automation.*

- Stand up store (Shopify + subscription app) with hero SKU (+ maybe 1).
- Connect supplier blind-ship / 3PL fulfillment.
- Set up payments (processor approved for supplements + backup).
- Build product page: third-party tested, **CoA published**, compliant
  structure/function copy + FDA disclaimer, reviews section, subscribe-and-save.
- Set up email/SMS (Klaviyo) with welcome + refill + win-back flows.
- Stand up support helpdesk with AI-draft (human-approved) replies.
- Implement the **claims-compliance gate** (file 06) into the content workflow.
- Plug numbers into `tools/unit_economics.py`; confirm ≥70% gross margin on paper.

> **GATE 1:** A test order flows end-to-end (checkout → supplier → blind-ship →
> tracking → subscription scheduled) flawlessly · all live copy passed the
> claims gate + human sign-off.

---

## Phase 2 — Validate demand on a small budget (Weeks 7–10)
*Goal: prove people will buy and subscribe — with real money, kept small.*

- Launch with a **capped** test ad budget on 1–2 channels + organic content.
- Brief any influencers/affiliates with a **compliant script + #ad disclosure**.
- Drive the funnel: capture → convert → onboard → subscribe.
- Measure relentlessly: CAC by channel, subscription conversion %, early churn
  signals, refund + chargeback rate.

> **GATE 2 (the big one):** With **real** data at small scale —
> **LTV:CAC ≥ 3**, **CAC payback < 90 days**, gross margin ≥ ~70%, refund +
> chargeback rates low. *If gates fail: stop scaling. Diagnose price / COGS /
> retention / channel and fix. Be willing to change niche or supplier. Do NOT
> "spend more to grow into it."*

---

## Phase 3 — Scale what works (Weeks 11–13+)
*Goal: pour fuel only on proven, profitable fire.*

- Increase budget **only** on channels that passed Gate 2, watching the metrics
  like a trading position — cut any channel that drifts below the gates.
- Deepen retention: optimise lifecycle flows, loyalty, bundles (highest-leverage
  profit lever — file 04).
- Consider moving the proven hero SKU from white-label to **private-label**
  (better margin + defensibility) and/or buying bulk inventory — only after
  weighing the cash and expiry trade-offs (file 03, 07).
- Add a **second SKU / bundle** only if it has a clear cross-sell to existing
  subscribers.
- Establish the weekly/monthly operating rhythm (file 07).

> **GATE 3 (scale responsibly):** Each new pound of spend still clears the
> economics gates · cash position safe · chargebacks low · supplier keeping up
> with no stockouts.

---

## The roadmap's governing principle

Each gate is a **"confirmed trend" check**. You only advance capital and effort
into the next phase when the current one is *confirmed* profitable and safe — never
on hope. This is what turns "a risky dropship gamble" into "a disciplined,
survivable business with real upside."

> **Honest expectation-setting:** most channels and even some niches will *fail*
> their gate on the first try — that's the system working, not failing. The
> small, capped losses from failed tests are the *price of information*. You
> keep your downside bounded, learn what works, and scale only the winners. That
> is how you stack the odds toward profit without ever betting the business.
