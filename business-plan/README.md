# The Plan: A Compliance-First Supplement Brand, Run Lean with AI

> **Read this first — an honest framing.**
> No one can *guarantee* profit or zero mistakes in a real business, and anyone
> who tells you otherwise is selling you something. What this plan *can* do is
> stack the odds heavily in your favour by doing the two things that actually
> separate supplement brands that survive from the ones that get sued, banned,
> or quietly bleed out:
>
> 1. **Treat regulation as the product, not an afterthought.** Supplements are
>    one of the most heavily policed consumer categories (FDA + FTC in the US,
>    MHRA/FSA + ASA in the UK/EU). Most failures here are *self-inflicted* —
>    illegal claims, mislabeled products, or a supplier that poisons people.
>    This plan removes those by design.
> 2. **Refuse to spend a pound you can't get back at a profit.** Same discipline
>    as the trading toolkit this repo started as: write the unit economics down,
>    prove them on a small budget, and never scale a channel that doesn't pay.

This is not a "get rich with dropshipping" pitch. It is a sober operating plan
for a **brand**, not a faceless reseller. The single biggest mistake in this
space is treating supplements like phone cases — random products, random
suppliers, illegal health claims. That gets you fined or hurts someone.

---

## The one-line strategy

> Build a **narrow, single-niche supplement brand** sold direct-to-consumer,
> fulfilled by a **vetted third-party (white-label / private-label dropship)
> manufacturer**, with **AI used to compress the cost of content, support, and
> operations** — and **subscriptions** as the profit engine.

Why this specific shape is the *safest profitable* version:

| Choice | Safer alternative we rejected | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **One niche, few SKUs** | Broad supplement store | Focus = trust, better margins, manageable compliance surface |
| **White-label from a US/EU GMP/cGMP facility** | Generic AliExpress dropship | You control quality, labels, and liability; ingestibles from unknown factories are a lawsuit waiting to happen |
| **Subscription-first** | One-off sales only | Recurring revenue is what makes the math work; CAC is paid back over months, not one order |
| **AI for leverage, human for trust** | Fully automated "passive income" | AI cuts cost; it does **not** write medical claims or run unsupervised |

See [`01-business-model.md`](01-business-model.md) for the full rationale.

## The non-negotiables (the "rules" — write them down, never improvise)

In the spirit of the trading toolkit: the edge is not any one rule, it's
following **all** of them, every time.

1. **No disease claims. Ever.** Supplements may not claim to cure, treat,
   prevent, or diagnose any disease. Marketing copy ships only after a
   claims-compliance check (file 02).
2. **No ingestible product from an unvetted facility.** Supplier must hold
   current GMP/cGMP certification and provide a Certificate of Analysis (CoA)
   per batch. No CoA, no sale.
3. **The brand owns the label and the customer.** We are not anonymous
   resellers; we control the SKU, the label, the data, and the relationship.
4. **No channel scales until it pays.** A marketing channel must show
   LTV > 3× CAC and CAC payback < 90 days at small scale before we add budget.
5. **AI drafts; a human approves anything customer-facing or health-related.**
6. **Cash discipline:** never pre-spend on inventory or ads beyond a loss we
   can absorb. Start with the dropship/print-on-demand model precisely so we
   don't sink capital into stock before demand is proven.

If a rule is broken to chase a sale, the plan has failed — stop and reset.

## What "safest" actually means here

Ranked by how often each one *kills* a supplement business, and how this plan
neutralises it:

1. **Regulatory / legal blow-up** → File 02 makes compliance the default path.
2. **Product harms someone** → Vetted GMP supplier + CoA + product liability
   insurance (file 02, 03).
3. **Negative unit economics at scale** → File 04 + the calculator force you to
   prove margins before scaling.
4. **Platform / ad-account ban** → Diversified channels + owned email/SMS list
   (file 05).
5. **Cash crunch from inventory** → Dropship/white-label first; buy inventory
   only after demand is proven (file 03, 07).

This is the same idea as the trading rules in this repo: **survival first, then
profit.** A business that can't be wiped out gets unlimited attempts to win.
