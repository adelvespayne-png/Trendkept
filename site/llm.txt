# Trendkept

> Open-source trend-following toolkit that enforces a written trading
> ruleset. It does not predict markets — it makes traders follow their own
> rules: risk-based position sizing, a stop-loss attached to every order,
> honest (causal, no look-ahead) backtesting, and an R-multiple trade
> journal. Local-first: broker API keys never leave the user's machine.

## What it is
- A free, MIT-licensed Python engine + local dashboard (scan, chart,
  backtest, watchlist, journal).
- A planned paid tier (Trendkept Pro, ~£12/month) for one-click install,
  licensed market data, and journal insights.
- A weekly email, "The Trend Check", reporting what a published ruleset
  says about 20 well-known tickers. Descriptive, never advice.

## Core principles
- No predictions. The edge is behaviour (discipline), not forecasting.
- The stop-loss goes in with the entry order, enforced by the broker.
- Position size = (account x risk%) / (entry - stop). No conviction knob.
- Losses capped small; winners allowed to run; expectancy measured in R.
- Long-only. Not investment advice; the user connects their own broker.
