# Trendkept — personal budget brief

*One page for personal-finance planning. Business context lives in the repo
(START_HERE.md / PLAN.md); figures from the honest model
(`business/model.py`, July 2026). All GBP. Update this file when the model
changes.*

## What this is

A side business (Trendkept: subscription software for hobbyist stock
traders) run solo at ~10 hours/week, ~2 h/week minimum viable. No staff, no
inventory, no borrowing. Personal liability is capped by a Ltd company from
month 2.

## Cash OUT of my pocket, by phase

| When | Out of pocket | What for |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 (validation) | **~£10–15 total** | Domain. Everything else on free tiers. |
| Month 2 (if day-30 signal is good) | **~£90 one-off** | Company registration £50 + registered office £40/yr |
| Month 3 (turning on payments) | **~£420 one-off** + monthly costs start | Legal review £300–500, ICO £40 |
| Months 3+ running costs | **~£350–450/month** | Data feed £60, insurance £25, hosting/admin/accounting ~£180, AI tooling ~£80, newsletter tier £0–25 |

- Months 1–2 are pre-revenue **by design** (nothing is for sale yet).
- **Worst-case year-one total if it earns nothing: ~£3,500–4,000** — but the
  plan's stop-gates mean a dead idea is abandoned at ~£15 (day 30) or ~£105
  (day 90), not £4,000.
- The AI tooling line (~£80/mo) may already be in my personal budget — don't
  double-count it.

## Cash IN, when and how much (three modelled outcomes)

| | Month 3 (launch) | Month 12 | Month 24 | Year 5 run-rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative (most likely)** | ~£5.5k one-off spike | ~£580/mo recurring | ~£1k/mo | ~£4–5k/mo |
| **Base (plan works)** | ~£17k one-off spike | ~£2.2k/mo | ~£5.4k/mo | ~£21k/mo |
| **Ambitious (breakout)** | ~£44k one-off spike | ~£7.8k/mo | ~£24k/mo | ~£95k/mo |

- The month-3 number is a **one-time launch spike** (lifetime deals) — treat
  it as a windfall, not a run-rate. It roughly repays year-one costs.
- Recurring revenue alone covers monthly costs from **~month 6–7** even in
  the conservative case (~35–40 subscribers = break-even).
- Long-term upside also includes selling the business (valued ~3× annual
  profit): base case crosses **£1M total value ~month 50**; conservative
  never does (~£240k at year 5).

## For paycheck planning, the three rules

1. **Don't budget any business income before month 3**, and none reliably
   before month 7. Treat the month-3 spike as savings, not spending money.
2. **Ring-fence the outgoings**: ~£15 (month 1), ~£90 (month 2), then up to
   ~£450/month from month 3 — ideally from a set-aside pot equal to
   ~£4,000 so the business never touches essential spending even in the
   worst case. All business money flows through the business bank account,
   never the personal one.
3. **Quit-the-day-job line is £5k/month recurring** (base case: ~month 24;
   conservative: not within 5 years). Until then this is a side income, and
   the day job stays.

## Time cost (it's the real investment)

~10 h/week steady (≈500 h/year), floor ~4–5 h/week, three launch weeks of
25+ h in year one. Only immovable slot: ~1 h every Sunday.
